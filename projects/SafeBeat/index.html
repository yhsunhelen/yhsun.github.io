Abstract

SafeBeat investigates an ECG monitoring wearable designed for older adults living independently, where adherence, comprehension, and comfort are as critical as measurement capability. The project frames ECG monitoring as a human-centered sensing system: the device captures a continuous physiological signal, while the software translates it into interpretable states intended to reduce cognitive burden and anxiety.

Technical contributions

  • Pipeline specification: a complete path from raw ECG to low-burden feedback (state + suggestion).
  • Interpretation strategy: rule-based baseline + trainable model option; both mapped to a simple alert language.
  • Evaluation plan: metrics focusing on false alarms, latency, and robustness under poor signal quality.

Context & Requirements

Problem framing and design constraints.

Clinical ECG monitoring tools often fail in everyday contexts due to friction (setup, comfort, interpretation), which is amplified for older adults who may not identify as “tech users.” SafeBeat therefore treats interpretability and sustained adoption as first-order design constraints.

Requirements (distilled)

  • Low-burden use: minimal steps, continuous capture, and stable wearing.
  • Interpretability: actionable states instead of raw charts; avoids medical intimidation.
  • Robustness: tolerates motion artifacts and imperfect contact; includes signal quality handling.
  • Trust: reduces anxiety by lowering false alarms and providing clear next-step guidance.

Feedback language (mapping)

Green: normal / stable · Orange: attention / re-check · Red: warning / rest & seek guidance (final thresholds and triggering rules are software-controlled and evaluated).

Signal Processing & Software

Prototype implementation: real-time acquisition, visualization, and model-based rhythm screening.

The software prototype validates an end-to-end ECG pipeline from streaming input to interpretable output. It supports real-time data acquisition over a serial interface with frame synchronization and checksum validation, parses incoming payloads into 16-bit ECG samples, and renders the waveform continuously for live monitoring. For rhythm screening, a trained model (loaded from a .mat file) runs online inference on sliding windows: instantaneous frequency (instfreq) and spectral entropy (pentropy) are extracted as a compact 2×T sequence feature, then minibatchpredict produces class scores and a predicted label, visualized as a confidence bar for quick inspection. The prototype also supports pausing/resuming the live stream and exporting recorded samples for offline review.
